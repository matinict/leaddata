

Canada (Elementary School) Math Concepts by Grade Level
Canada’s math curriculum varies slightly by province (Ontario, Quebec, British Columbia, Alberta, etc.), but core concepts are generally similar across the country.

Kindergarten
    • Counting numbers (0–20+)
    • Number recognition
    • Basic patterns
    • Sorting and classifying objects
    • Shapes (circle, square, triangle, rectangle)
    • Measurement basics (big/small, long/short)
    • Simple addition/subtraction with objects
    • Time basics (day/night, routines)

Grade 1
    • Counting to 100
    • Addition and subtraction within 20
    • Place value (ones, tens)
    • Comparing numbers
    • Basic fractions (halves)
    • 2D and 3D shapes
    • Measurement (length, weight)
    • Money recognition (coins)
    • Time to the hour/half-hour
    • Graphing basics

Grade 2
    • Addition/subtraction to 100
    • Skip counting (2s, 5s, 10s)
    • Place value to hundreds
    • Introduction to multiplication
    • Fractions (halves, thirds, quarters)
    • Measurement using standard units
    • Time to 5 minutes
    • Money counting
    • Data collection and graphs
    • Geometry and symmetry

Grade 3
    • Multiplication/division basics
    • Place value to thousands
    • Fractions
    • Perimeter
    • Area basics
    • Mass, capacity, temperature
    • Time and calendars
    • Money and making change
    • Bar graphs and pictographs
    • Probability basics

Grade 4
    • Multiplication/division fluency
    • Multi-digit operations
    • Decimals (tenths, hundredths)
    • Fractions equivalency
    • Angles
    • Area and perimeter
    • Measurement conversions
    • Data analysis
    • Probability
    • Patterns and algebra introduction

Grade 5
    • Decimals operations
    • Fractions operations
    • Percent introduction
    • Volume
    • Coordinate grids
    • Geometry (angles, polygons)
    • Ratios
    • Probability
    • Graphing
    • Financial literacy basics

Grade 6
    • Ratios, rates, percentages
    • Integers introduction
    • Algebraic expressions
    • Variables
    • Surface area and volume
    • Geometry transformations
    • Data management
    • Probability
    • Order of operations
    • Financial math

Provincial Notes
    • Ontario: Strong focus on coding, financial literacy, and social-emotional learning in math
    • Quebec: Emphasizes problem-solving and numeracy
    • British Columbia: Competency-based with applied reasoning
    • Alberta: Traditional structure with strong arithmetic progression
